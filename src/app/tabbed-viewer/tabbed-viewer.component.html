Getting Started Days One through Three Resources At all times, if the player has necessary tools and no higher priority, they should try to harvest resources as these will always be depleted. This includes:
  • Cut grass
  • Twigs
  • Logs
  • Stone
  • Nitre (Kate calls this nitrate)
  • Flint
  • Gold
  • While this is the least important to regularly gather, it can still be useful to have on hand.
  • Marble
Food Food is a necessary component of the game, as suggested by the name; however, not all food is created equally. Crockpot
  • The crockpot is easily the most valuable resource in the game. This will create high benefit foods when four items are combined. The recipes below should regularly work:
  • One meat + one monster meat + two filler
  • One monster meat + one red cap + two filler (Note: this is a mod)
  • Three honey + one filler
  • One meat + three filler
  • One meat + one filler + two ice
  • Note: frog legs count as meat for the above recipes.
  • Note: some recipes will be inedible, such as four ice, four butterflies, or a dish with more than one monster meat.
    •  Filler Forage should not be eaten unless completely out of food and away from camp.
  • Forage
  • This includes, cooked or otherwise:
  • Berries
  • Carrots
  • Birch nuts
  • Mushrooms
  • Mushrooms can screw you over real quick. Cooked green caps will quickly restore sanity. Raw green and raw red caps will hurt you real fast.
  • If you're away from camp and out of prepared food, cooking these items over a campfire will provide ample nutrition. However, considering these foods provide more benefit when combined with others, this is only to be done in an emergency situation.
  • Crops
  • These come from either farms or (if Wormwood is in game) sown in the ground. If farms are utilized, these are very slow to grow and resource intensive. Only upgraded farms should be used. If grown from the ground, Wormwood has to spend time and resources growing these.

  • Hound Attacks
  • Hounds attack regularly starting typically on the eighth day of the game. These attacks start out small and will grow as the game progresses. Conveniently, the character will announce the oncoming prescence once the hounds become loud; they are audible before this if you listen carefully. If Wendy is present, try to lead the hounds to Abigail and let her take most of the attacks. Otherwise, additional approaches include:
  • Befriending pig-men by giving them monster meat. They will in turn defend you.
  • Leading the hounds to a spider's den.
  • Leading the hounds to a Beefalo herd. Regardless of the Beefalo's agro status, they serve as quality tanks against the hounds and will fight back.
  • Leading the hounds to a Treeguard. This is a little weak in my opinion but you do you.
  • Hound Mounds
  • Hounds naturally occur in some deserts among large piles of bones. You can kill them but they just keep spawning. Best just to go home as they'll stop following you eventually.
  • Red Hound
  • The red hounds occur typically in summer and will ignite when killed. They are the reason for Kate's fear of fighting any hounds at camp: sometimes any hound ignites for zero reason.
  • White Hound
  • The white hounds have to go with freezing or something. They're mostly winter creatures. You can't kill them with an ice staff or something.

  • These tend to be the most useful creatures as they're the main source of gears, along with tumbleweeds and graves. They're fairly strong, but two players in log armor with spears should be able to take them out with little trouble.

  • Frogs
  • These are awful and can gang up real quick. Best to stay away from large clusters of ponds unless Wendy is in game or the player is feeling cocky. Frog ponds freeze in winter.
  • Spiders
  • Useful. Not too bad except the warriors. Abigail has no trouble unless three or more dens attack at once. Silk and spider glands are incredibly useful.
  • Tallbird
  • If you can get her egg, it doesn't spoil and makes for a good back up food in the winter. With log armor she won't hurt you much and will only follow you two screens worth before returning to her nest.
